Authors

Paulo Coelho

Paulo Coelho is a Brazilian author known for his profound and philosophical writing,
often exploring themes of spirituality, destiny, and self-discovery.
Coelho's writing style is often characterized by its simplicity and depth, making complex ideas accessible to a broad audience.

J K Rowling

J. K. Rowling, is a British author and philanthropist. She is the author of Harry Potter, a seven-volume fantasy novel series published from 1997 to 2007. The Casual Vacancy (2012) was her first novel for adults.

Chetan Bhagat

Chetan Bhagat born 22 April 1974 is an Indian author Five of his novels have been adapted into films.His novels focus on youth and urban India. making him popular among urban audiences

Stephen King

Stephen Edwin King is an American author. Widely known for his horror novels, he has been crowned the "King of Horror". He has also explored other genres, among them suspense, crime, science-fiction, fantasy and mystery.[

Arundhathi Roy

Suzanna Arundhati Roy is an Indian author best novel of her is The God of Small Things, which won the Booker Prize for Fiction in 1997 and became the best-selling book by Indian author.

Agatha Christie

She was a British author known for her 66 detective novels and 14 short story collections, particularly those revolving around fictional detectives Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple. She also wrote the world's longest-running play, the murder mystery The Mousetrap, which has been performed in the West End of London since 1952.

Alex Michaelides

Michaelides was born in Cyprus to a Greek Cypriot father and English mother. He studied psychotherapy for three years, and worked for two years at Northgate Clinic Adolescent unit.
This work provided material and inspiration for his debut novel The Silent Patient.
